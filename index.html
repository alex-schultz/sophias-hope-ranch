About Us

My Story

Welcome to the heart of Sophia’s Hope Ranch, a place born from a profound personal journey and a deep-seated commitment to transforming the lives of children and families. My name is Theresa Schultz, and my path as a devoted Christ-follower, mother, and clinical therapist has led me to this pivotal moment. With five grown children—two adopted, three biological—and three wonderful grandchildren, my life has been a tapestry woven with love, advocacy, and unyielding dedication to nurturing children in need.

For over two decades, I have channeled my passion for advocating for children into various roles, from serving on numerous boards to fostering over a dozen children and dedicating 12 years as a CASA. My professional journey as a clinical therapist specializing in trauma and attachment has been both challenging and rewarding, shaping my understanding of the profound needs of children and families navigating their own struggles.

The inception of Sophia’s Hope Ranch is deeply personal. When I first adopted my daughter at nine months old, her struggles with Reactive Attachment Disorder were heartbreaking and isolating. I felt the weight of her pain and the absence of sufficient resources to support her effectively. Determined to change this reality, I delved into research and sought out every possible avenue for support. The journey was arduous, but it ignited a fire within me to ensure that no other family would face such trials alone.

With the passage of time and the accumulation of resources, I am finally able to bring this dream to fruition. Sophia’s Hope Ranch stands as a testament to the belief that even amidst our deepest struggles, God’s grace and redemption can turn pain into purpose. We are committed to offering healing, support, and comfort to those who need it most, guided by the conviction that what was once meant for harm can be transformed into something profoundly good.

Join us as we embark on this mission to create a sanctuary of hope and healing, where every child and family can find solace, strength, and the unwavering support they deserve.

Mission, Vision, and Values

Mission Statement

Sophia’s Hope Treatment Center is dedicated to transforming families affected by attachment disruption through an interdisciplinary treatment approach. we recognize that therapy is not a one size fits all and will utilize various innovative, evidence-based practices to inspire connection, health, and well-being in each family served.

Vision Statement

We aim to see families and children create meaningful and sustainable attachments to establish a positive future outlook.

Pillars of Healing

  • Faith: Trusting in Jesus as the source of true healing.
  • Connection: Fostering relationships.
  • Surrender: Embracing God’s plan.
  • Responsibility: Growth and healing.

Our Services

  • Individual Therapy: For children, parents, and siblings.
  • Family Therapy: Strengthening bonds.
  • Intensive Outpatient Program: For children ages 2+.
  • Residential Program: For children ages 5–17.
